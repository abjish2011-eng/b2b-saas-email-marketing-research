## Post Text

We’ve all heard this prophecy before: all jobs are going away, we are automating everything, and soon you will belong to a permanent underclass. 

Matan Grinberg (
@matanSF
) thinks this prediction does real harm to the psychology of people who still have an important role to play in the future of humanity.

He objects that the world contains far more unsolved problems than people solving them, and most could be addressed with software if anyone bothered to write it. That's because better quality of life does not necessarily end old problems. It manufactures new ones, each generation inheriting expectations the last could not have imagined.

"We don't need to live like this," he said.

## Newsletter Text

Hi friends,
Happy Friday and welcome to our 200th Weekly Dose of Optimism.
A full 200 weeks after Dan and I started writing the Weekly Dose at the depth of the despair market in July 2022, the optimism is at an all-time high around these parts. We write this 200th Dose to you on the eve of America’s 250th birthday, just two days after the USMNT beat Bosnia and Herzegovina and the Sixers (finally) fleeced the Celtics in a trade.
America feels like it’s on fire, because it’s way too hot outside for one, but more importantly because the energy around the World Cup has been exquisite, we’ve played the role of host to the world well, the markets are soaring, and everyone’s a little happier in the summer.
Since we started the Dose four years ago, obesity has been cured, cars have begun driving themselves commercially, AI has become very smart and useful, there has been a renaissance of atoms companies in America, and multiple new advanced nuclear reactors have gone critical. That was just a quick list off the dome; if you’ve been following along, you know that practically every week is that jam-packed.

This week is no different.

(1) Conception Bio Generates First Early Human Eggs from Stem Cells
The team at Conception, a startup in Berkeley, says it has grown the first early human egg cells from stem cells. To do it, they drew blood, reprogrammed the blood cells into induced pluripotent stem cells, and coaxed those into miniature human ovaries that in turn grow the eggs inside them.
The early eggs went through meiosis and support cells wrapped each one in a follicle, recreating the defining structure of a real developing ovary.
The post says this could eventually allow for two biological fathers, better embryo selection, extended fertility, and the possibility of de-extincting more animals.

(2) SpudCell, the first synthetic cell with a complete life cycle
Scientists at the University of Minnesota created the first synthetic cell assembled entirely from non-living chemicals that can feed, grow, reproduce, and compete for food.
The newsletter says the work suggests we may be able to begin engineering life from scratch instead of starting with a living cell.

(3) Meta releases v2 of its mind-reading model
Packy notes Meta released a new version of a model that can read minds from outside the brain and translate brain activity into words.

(4) Nuclear race update
The issue gives a quick roundup of nuclear milestones, including fuel delivery, reactors beginning to split atoms, and multiple companies reaching criticality or moving toward it.

(5) America’s Next 250
The issue closes by pointing readers to Scott Nolan’s essay on how much progress America has made in its first 250 years and what it will take to do even more in the next 250.
